#+title: Engineering Management

* Servant leadership
- The leader's job is to serve the team: remove obstacles, build consensus, fill cracks — not to "manage" in the traditional sense
- Best advice from a Google engineering director: /Above all, resist the urge to manage/
#+begin_quote
Traditional managers worry about how to get things done, whereas great managers worry about what things get done (and trust their team to figure out how to do it).
#+end_quote
- Google avoids the Peter Principle ("in a hierarchy every employee rises to their level of incompetence") by requiring engineers to already perform at the level above before being promoted to it

* Leading at scale: always be deciding, leaving, scaling
- *Always be deciding*: at scale, the job is finding right trade-offs, not solving specific engineering tasks; identify blinders, identify key trade-offs, decide and iterate; avoid analysis paralysis by framing decisions as revisitable
- *Always be leaving*: build a self-driving team so the problem is solved without you present; anchor teams to *problems* not *solutions* ("we own version control" not "we own the Git repos"); if you can't take a week off without checking email, you are the single point of failure
- *Always be scaling*: the cycle of success brings harder problems with no new people; distinguish important vs. urgent (Eisenhower); learn to drop balls deliberately — only the top 20% critical things truly require you; protect energy (real vacations ≥1 week, true disconnection)
