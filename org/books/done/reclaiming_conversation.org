* About this book
Sherry Turkle argues that the always-on, device-mediated life is eroding our capacity for face-to-face conversation — and with it, empathy, creativity, and genuine intimacy. Drawing on years of interviews across families, schools, and workplaces, she shows how the flight from conversation to connection has left people lonelier, less self-aware, and less capable of the kind of sustained, vulnerable talk that builds real relationships. The book is both a diagnosis and a call to reclaim solitude and conversation as foundations of a fully human life.
