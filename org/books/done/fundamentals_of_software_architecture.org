* About this book

A comprehensive, practical guide to software architecture covering the architect's role, architectural characteristics, structural patterns, and soft skills. The central thesis: architecture is not about finding perfect solutions but about understanding trade-offs. Grounded in three laws:

1. *Everything in software architecture is a trade-off.* (First Law)
2. *Why is more important than how.* (Second Law)
3. *Most architecture decisions aren't binary but rather exist on a spectrum between extremes.* (Third Law)

* Key concepts

- [[id:5a26ed6a-80d8-4fe8-b9db-0c1956ec8c2e][Software Architecture]] — four dimensions: style, characteristics, components, decisions; architecture is about leaving options open and minimising lifetime cost
- [[id:ef58fefe-68ae-4e56-8996-202139c6d27d][Software Architect]] — eight core expectations: make decisions, continually analyse, stay current, ensure compliance, understand technology, know the domain, lead, navigate politics
- [[id:c02a0524-7970-43bb-b19c-49994bdf612b][Architectural thinking]] — architecture vs design spectrum; trade-off analysis; Frozen Caveman antipattern; the 20-minute breadth rule
- [[id:9d0894b3-9a94-4b21-bba9-ca0489604329][Knowledge pyramid]] — stuff you know / stuff you know you don't know / stuff you don't know you don't know; architects optimise for breadth over depth
- [[id:4b4d8324-afd8-49ff-9f19-b5bde5b02022][Modularity]] — cohesion types (1–7), LCOM metric, abstractness/instability/distance from main sequence; connascence as a richer coupling vocabulary
- [[id:e3ea3021-3adb-4611-abca-ce0cce3cb27c][Coupling]] — afferent vs efferent; Zone of Pain (concrete + stable) and Zone of Uselessness (abstract + unstable)
- [[id:bd8e9470-33d5-4c7d-a9ba-31adc7bf1e18][Connascence]] — static (Name, Type, Meaning, Position, Algorithm) and dynamic (Execution, Timing, Values, Identity); strength, locality, degree properties; Weirich's two rules
- [[id:f9912f2f-5861-469f-816e-0765a78687b1][Architectural characteristics]] — the "-ilities"; three criteria; four categories (operational, structural, cloud, cross-cutting); implicit vs explicit; Vasa antipattern; least-worst architecture principle
- [[id:b51a2811-2fe3-4f03-a8c0-ff4199376e17][Architecture katas]] — structured practice exercises; Silicon Sandwiches and Going-Going-Gone worked examples; domain-concern translation table
- [[id:953cf8e0-8716-4288-a304-08be0f5c6931][Fitness functions]] — automated/manual × continuous/triggered; JDepend, ArchUnit, NetArchTest, chaos engineering; the enforcement arm of evolutionary architecture
- [[id:575d9629-d5b9-41c4-9868-2e864400fa0c][Architectural governance]] — governing decisions through automated checks rather than manual reviews; checklist manifesto framing
